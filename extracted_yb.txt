CERTIFICATION: FOUR ALPHA YELLOW BELT (Body of Knowledge)

MODULE 1: Advanced DMAIC & The SIPOC Architecture

The Transition from Theory to Practice: Moving from identifying the 3Ds (Delay, Defect, Deviation) to actively mapping the processes that cause them.

SIPOC for Human Behavior: Adapting the Supplier-Input-Process-Output-Customer tool to biological units:

Supplier: Circumstances {C}) and System rules {S}).

Input: Energy, Time, Attention, and Capital (alpha1).

Process: The specific daily habit or behavioral loop.

Output: The resulting Gross or Subtle metric.

Customer: The Future Self, the Family, or the Community.

Value-Stream Mapping (VSM): Categorizing every daily action as Value-Adding (increases N*), Non-Value-Adding but Necessary (basic maintenance), or Pure Waste (the 3Ds).

MODULE 2: The Science of Measurement & Data Integrity

Operational Definitions: Learning to define subjective states objectively. (e.g., Instead of "I want better alpha2 harmony," defining it as "Zero raised-voice arguments and 30 minutes of uninterrupted dialogue daily.")

Data Collection Plans: How to track alpha1 --> alpha4 metrics accurately over 30 days without observer bias.

Understanding Variation: Differentiating between Common Cause Variation (normal daily mood fluctuations) and Special Cause Variation (a severe friction event triggering an A-Y Defect).

MODULE 3: Micro-RCTs (Evidence-Based Life Design)

The J-PAL Integration: Translating Banerjee and Duflo’s macro-economic RCT methodology into micro-behavioral interventions.

The A/B Testing of Human Potential: Instead of permanently adopting a new, untested habit based on assumption (the A-Y Defect), the Yellow Belt runs a controlled trial.

Control Phase: Logging baseline N* without the intervention.

Treatment Phase: Applying the CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) intervention.

Evaluation: Measuring the true delta (Delta N*) to ensure the intervention actually caused the improvement, isolating it from random external shocks (dW).

Falsification: Learning to accept when a highly desired strategy mathematically fails to improve the N* score.

MODULE 4: Root Cause Analytics & The Law of Motion

Advanced 5 Whys & Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagrams: Plotting complex, multi-variable friction points across the Four Alphas. If alpha3 (Status/Career) is failing, mapping the contributing factors from environment, methods, mental bandwidth, and biological health.

Calculating the SDE Variables: Quantifying the dVh = F + M + dW equation in real life. Identifying exact metrics for F (Control Input), M (Interdependence friction from spouse/boss), and dW (Random life shocks).

MODULE 5: Behavioral Mistake-Proofing (Poka-Yoke)

Designing the Constraint: Relying on Axiom 4 (Controllable Constraints). Instead of trying to "be better," the Yellow Belt physically alters their environment to make the defect impossible.

Example: If the alpha4 defect is digital distraction, the Poka-Yoke is an automated router shut-off at 9:00 PM.

The Control Plan: Establishing a personal visual management dashboard. Creating daily leading indicators (tracking the input) rather than lagging indicators (waiting for the output).

MODULE 6: The Dwivedi-Nash Audit

The Ethical Impact Assessment: Before concluding any Yellow Belt project, conducting a rigorous audit to prove that the personal optimization (Delta N* > 0) did not secretly offload friction onto a spouse, team member, or community system (min(N*{post}) geq min(N*{pre})).
